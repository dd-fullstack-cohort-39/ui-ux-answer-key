Interaction Flow: Creating a Post On dev.to

Katy wants to be able to write and share an article she just wrote about Angular

Precondition

Katy has an account on dev.to, has created articles before, and is logged in.

Postcondition

Katy can easily find her article and can share it to her social media.

Interaction Flow

  • Katy scans the home page and clicks on the write a post button in the navbar.
  • dev.to takes her to the new page and displays a markdown editor of Katy to use.
  • Katy clicks into the header section
  • dev.to focuses on the Title input field
  • Katy writes her title
  • katy clicks on the tags input field
  • dev.to focuses on the tags input field
  • Katy begins typing ang
  • dev.to opens an autocomplete dialog and recommends angular
  • Katy clicks on angular
  • Katy clicks on the content field
  • dev.to focuses on the content text area
  • Katy writes markdown and adds a liquid link to her linkedin
  • Katy proof reads her article and clicks the preview button
  • dev.to shows her the rendered version of her post
  • katy clicks on edit and makes a few changes
  • Katy clicks publish to finalize her post
  • dev.to publishes her post and adds it to its newsfeed queuing algorithm
